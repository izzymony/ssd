FAQ'S

  • Yes, Penpot offers a self-hosted option. Self-hosting allows individuals and organizations to deploy Penpot on their own servers, giving them complete control over their data and integration with internal systems​. Penpot is Open Source, and self-hosting Penpot will be free forever.

  • We’re considering an additional paid tier for teams who don’t need enterprise-level setup and configuration. This plan would include premium support, the ability to select your preferred hosting region, and more. We won't paywall existing features (see below!) Please get in touch if you’re interested in exploring this plan.

  • At Penpot we don’t believe in restricting users by paywalling features. We’re most likely to follow a “tax the controller” model, where we create paid tiers aimed at medium to large organizations that need specific configurations. Power users don’t typically need these features and will enjoy Penpot free forever.

  • We founded Penpot in 2019 as an open-source project. In 2021, we received external funding from several Spanish business leaders. We then raised a seed round with Athos Capital 9 and CDTI Innvierte, along with self-funding from employee savings. In 2022, we raised an $8m series A round, led by Decibel, followed by Athos, and supported by a group of super-angels. All of our investors are deeply committed to Penpot’s Open Source DNA, and we wouldn’t have it any other way.

  • Multiple organizations are already using Penpot as their primary design tool. We’ve started discussions with these organizations to align our future offerings with their needs, but we are not yet charging for any services. Our current focus is building a robust product that meets the community's expectations before introducing paid tiers​.

  • A certified build is a premium version of Penpot tailored for enterprise users. It includes advanced features for legal and security compliance. We’ve designed these builds to meet the needs of medium to large organizations that require enhanced compliance, control, and security.

  • Penpot was built from the ground up as an Open Source product using open standards. You will always be able to take your design files and import them into your tool of choice, as long as it supports open file standards.
